About Frank Gehry

Frank Gehry was born in Canada on February 28, 1929 and had studied at the University of Southern California and Harvard University. He is one of the most renowned architects of the 20th century, famous for his bold, sculptural and unusual composition. He became a prominent figure in the deconstructionist and postmodern styles of architecture. His design style encompasses irregular forms and radical, expressive forms.

Famous Work: Guggenheim Museum Bilbao

The Guggeheim Museum is a museum of modern and contemporary art located in Bilbao, Basque Country, Spain. The museum presents permanent and visiting exhibits of works by Spanish and international artists. The museum was considered as one of the most important work completed since 1980 in the 2010 World Architecture Survey among professional architects.

Materials Used

The external layer of the structure is made of titanium after careful consideration after seeing the behavior of a titanium sample attached outside his office. The facade is clad with a total of 42, 875 titanium plates which had undergone various stages of refinement and transported from large distances before they were assembled. The conclusion of the journey produced extremely thin titanium plates used in the building construction. In addition to titanium, two additional materials were utilized for the twisted exterior facades. The first are slabs of limestone mined in Granada and meticulously polished before installation, and the second are thermal double-glazed glass sheets.
