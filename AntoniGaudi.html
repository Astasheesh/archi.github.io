About Antoni Gaudi

Spanish architect Antoni Gaudi developed a style all his own. Born in 1852 in the Catalonia region of Spain, Gaudi was a fervent Catholic who believed that he could glorify God by deriving his inspiration from nature, God's creation. Gaudi favored curves rather than straight lines, varied textures and vibrant colors. His unique and somewhat bizarre style was part neo-Gothic, part avant-garde, part surrealistic. The Sagrada Familia in Barcelona stands as his most famous work. However, the cathedral was unfinished at his death in 1926.

Famous Work: The Sagrada Familia

Antoni Gaudí created the design for this little Roman Catholic Basilica in Barcelona, Spain. One of Barcelona's most well-known structures, the Sagrada Família was started in 1882 and is still unfinished in the first quarter of the twenty-first century. It is renowned for its tactile organic form. The Sagrada Familia’s exterior is a masterpiece of symbolism and nature-inspired design. Its three façades—Nativity, Passion, and Glory—depict key moments of Christ’s life, from his birth to resurrection. The tall spires that make up the Sagrada Família's towers symbolize important biblical. characters including Jesus, Mary, the Apostles, and the Evangelists. Reaching for the sky, these towers are meant to represent trust.
