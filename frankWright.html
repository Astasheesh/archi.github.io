Frank Lloyd Wright

Pioneer of Organic Architecture

About Frank Lloyd Wright

Frank Lloyd Wright was an American architect known for his innovative designs and organic architecture. He designed over 1,000 structures, with 532 completed. Wright's philosophy of "organic architecture" emphasized harmony between human habitation and the natural world.

Famous Work: Fallingwater House

Fallingwater, designed in 1935 and completed in 1937, is a house built over a waterfall in Pennsylvania. It is renowned for its integration with the natural landscape and its innovative design.

The house exemplifies Wright's concept of organic architecture, with its dramatic cantilevered balconies and integration with the surrounding forest and waterfall. Rather than building a house with a view of the waterfall, Wright built the house on top of it, making it part of the residents' everyday experience.

Materials Used

The house primarily uses natural materials such as stone, concrete, and glass. The stone was quarried from the site itself, and the concrete was used for the cantilevered terraces.

  • Stone: Local sandstone quarried from the site
  • Concrete: Used for the iconic cantilevered terraces
  • Glass: Large windows to connect interior spaces with nature
  • Steel: Reinforcement for the concrete cantilevers
